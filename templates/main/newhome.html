About Paspasha!

Paspasha is a Sanskrit word and the name of the first chapter of the book ‘Patanjal-Mahabhashya’ composed by the great grammarian Patanjali. The word Paspasha is derived from a root verb ‘spash’ which means to touch. The chapter is known as Paspasha as the grammarian has ‘touched’ or discussed and dealt with various aspects of nature, language, and culture in this chapter.

At Paspasha, we teach languages, history, art, and cultures while we touch upon different areas of life. We will teach you how history affects our society today, and help you connect the dots between evolution of culture and language. We will teach essential skills along with the grammar and conversation skills in our language courses. This is the approach a child takes to learn a new language and thus is the apt way to learn for non native speakers too!

We continue to work really hard to get some super passionate tutors to teach you their native cultures, histories, and languages. Learning from these talented young women and men would allow you to truly understand and grasp a culture and it's language, while giving an international platform to the traditions of these young tutors.
