Syllabus

Ancient Indian literature, scripts, stories, sociology, art, and architecture. Paspasha course is tailor made for students who want a great crash course about Vedic India!

In four classes of one hour each, get ready to learn about Vedic literature, ancient scripts, mythology, and academic theories!

    1. Introduction to Vedic literature, and evolution of Hindu Gods.

      2. Get introduced to the jargon of Vedic literature, that is the four Vedas and their ancillary texts. We will understand the process and ideas behind the creation of all these texts, their subject matter, historical timeline, and influence they have on the Indian culture.
      Learn about the evolution of Hindu Gods from the Vedas to the current period. We will learn how their importance, hierarchy, and significance changed in the course of time. We will understand the iconography of Hindu Gods, their stories, and thoughts behind all their imagery.

    2. Classical Sanskrit Literature

      3. The Sanskrit language has acquired its greatness because of the amazing classical literature created using this language over the centuries. In this class we will give an introduction about famous Sanskrit plays and poems like the pancha-mahakavyas, brillant Sanskrit authors and playwrights like Kalidasa and doctrines of philosophies like the six darshanas. Overall this lecture will leave you awestruck, seeing the creativity, intellect, and capabilities of our ancestors.

    3. Ancient Indian Script - Brahmi

      4. Brahmi is the most ancient Indian deciphered script. In this course, we will learn about the development of this script, various epigraphs of important kings like Ashoka around India which have been inscribed in this script and of course the most fun part will be learning to read and write it.

    4. Temple Architecture of ancient India

      5. In this course we will learn in detail about the architecture of important ancient Indian temples. We will also learn about the ancient systems of temple construction and understand more about the iconography of Gods and decorative artwork around the temple.
