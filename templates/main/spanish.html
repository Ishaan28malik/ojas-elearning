About Komal!

Komal is a young tutor, a daughter of Indian expats, born and brought up in Spain. She spent a few years in India teaching Spanish while she studied for her undergraduate degree at Symbiosis University. Through the years Komal has been teaching Spanish and English and must have taught 1000+ students over hundreds of hours!
At Paspasha, we teach languages while we touch upon different areas of life and teach essential skills along with the grammar and conversation skills. This is the approach a child takes to learn a new language and thus is the apt way to learn for non native speakers too!

Please see Our Syllabus to know how we teach!
Syllabus

Learn to cook, garden, and debate, as you learn a new language!

At Paspasha, our syllabus is divided into three distinct parts and is taught by native speakers of the language.

    1: Skill Development:

    Our tutors teach essential life skills like making tea, cooking ramen, packing their bags for a vacation, in a language the student has chosen. Native speakers of every language learn to speak well when their parents teach and show them different aspects of life. We want our students to learn a new language as if they are living the language. This ensures they retain it well, and learn it faster.

    2: Formal Grammar training:

    We cover formal grammar training in three different levels: beginner, medium, and advanced. Please see the syllabus below for a more detailed description.

    3: Debate and expressing oneself:

    We help students learn the art of expressing their opinions in the language they are learning. From Global warming to Gender equality, we will help the students frame arguments, look for sources online, and argue effectively.

Section One: Skill development

Learning life skills with the language you choose!

The idea behind this section of our course is to provide an environment for students to learn a new language as they acquire life skills. It is this approach which helps native speakers learn a language. It is with daily communication with family and friends that one picks up a language. We will thus create a classroom environment which will give students a chance to learn how to cook simple dishes, pack their bags for a vacation, tidy their rooms, and plant saplings.

Two days before a class, the students will get a detailed worksheet explaining what they will be learning in class. This worksheet will have all the important words that will be used in class and students can try and remember them.

A class will last for 60 minutes. The tutor will slowly explain and animate every step. The students are expected to listen and follow. Once the entire activity is done, the tutor will revise it again and take the students through all the steps. Thus the students will hear the words and sentences at least two times after reading them in the worksheet before class.

Below listed is a framework in which these classes will operate.


Cooking:

Vocabulary - 60+ unique words

  1. Boil water.

    2. This simple daily activity will be shown with great detail. Our tutors will pronounce and show every action animated and slow. It is with this simple recipe, that we will go on to learn more complex recipes. Words learnt would range from ‘water’, and ‘utensils’ to ‘bubbles in water’.

  2. Make tea / coffee / lemonade.

    3. Once a student has learnt to boil water, they can proceed to learn how to make tea. This step would familiarise them with words like ‘sugar’, ‘milk’ etc.

    Similar recipes like preparing coffee, lemonade, fruit juices will follow. Students will learn words for different fruits, salt, ice etc.

  3. Cook eggs.

    4. For students who do eat eggs, we will teach three different ways to cook eggs. They will learn words for omelettes, boiled eggs, scrambled eggs, different vegetables, cheese and butter.

  4. Make ramen/maggi.

    5. This is another easy skill every future college student needs to acquire! We will prepare our students to cook really delicious ramen with different variations.

  5. Preparing a salad.

    6. We will teach students words for different vegetables, sauces, sprouts etc.

  6. More complex recipes will follow.

    7. We will consult our students and then decide which recipes to go ahead with. Our tutors are from Latin America, Europe, and India so they are really really good at a lot of mouth watering recipes.

We would help the students become super skilled waiters of the house too. Every dish they prepare would be served to a family member. We will teach them how to serve well, and be a polite waiter. It will equip them with formal vocabulary which one uses to speak with clients.

Packing your bags for a vacation:

Vocabulary : 60+ unique words.

This is an important life skill which our students would need as they graduate and go on to attend college. We will teach the best way to ensure your clothes fit, to make a list of all essential items, and ensure they are travel ready!

  1. Folding clothes and fitting items in a bag.

    2. This simple exercise uses a lot of words, from ‘jeans’, ‘shirts’, ‘innerwears’, ‘sweaters’, to ‘toothbrush’ and other ‘toiletries’. As we slowly and carefully take our students through the process of packing their bags, they begin to remember these words for a lifetime.

  2. Preparing your important documents.

    3. International travel and in many cases domestic travel needs you to carry important documents with you. We will help the students create a list of important documents like passports, driving licence, baggage tags etc so they learn a lot more words! This is also essential vocabulary since during their travels, these words will help them greatly in times of emergency or distress.

  3. Browsing internet websites from a different language, and making hotel, taxi bookings.

    4. We will help the students navigate a website in a language they are learning, and help understand vocabulary about booking a taxi, paying foreign currency, and speaking over the phone to someone who speaks the language they are learning.

Cleaning your room:

Vocabulary: 50+ unique words.

Through this module we will explore different parts of a room, vocabulary for cleaning, organising, and decorating.

  1. Organise your shelf and desk.

    2. We will teach our students the vocabulary for ‘dust’, ‘wiping’, ‘clean’, ‘dirty’, ‘stacking books’ etc.

  2. Making your bed.

    3. We will teach the vocabulary for bed sheets, pillows, blankets etc.

  3. Decorating your room.

    4. We will teach our students vocabulary for different decorations they would like to have for their room. From posters, fairy lights to flowers we will cover it all!

Art and craft:

Vocabulary: 50+ unique words.

We will cover small paper plane models, simple origami, making cartoons, stick figure drawings etc.
Words covered will include words for different types of paper, cardboard, glue, colours, pens etc.

Gardening:

Vocabulary: 20+ unique words.

In this part we will cover sowing two to three different seeds. We will cover activities like watering a plant, feeding it manure, removing weeds etc.

Music and other arts:

Vocabulary: 80+ unique words.

Once our students have completed the above modules of our Skill Development section, we offer opportunities to learn the Guitar, the Flute, the Chello etc in the language they have chosen.

Our courses have been carefully designed to ensure what you learn remains with you for a long time, and that you come out of each lecture exposed to new ideas. Check our courses and our teaching style details.

Section Two: Formal Grammar training.

Learn sentence construction, tenses, and other grammar rules!

In this section students will learn Grammar and other essential components of mastering a language. We have divided this section in three parts, and students can choose any one based on their knowledge level.

The beginners part, expects no previous knowledge of the language and we will introduce everything from the very basics.

The medium level expects some familiarity with the grammar of the language.

The advanced level expects significant familiarity and comfort with the rules of grammar and conversational skills.

Below given is the syllabus for section two for students of Spanish language. For other languages this section will change slightly.

20 Hour Beginner Course:


  1. Alphabet, rules, basic questions, greetings, numbers 0 to 10.
  2. Days of the week, months.
  3. Numbers till 1 Million, articles with class objects.
  4. Personal Pronouns. Verbs: ser, tener, llamarse.
  5. Vocabulary: Nationalities and professions.
  6. Regular verbs (- AR, - ER, - IR) with practice.
  7. Por, para, porque. Verb: Querer.
  8. Prepositions of place. Vocabulary: Family tree, marital status.
  9. Possessives with practice.
  10. Adjectives to describe a person.
  11. Verbs: Gustar, encantar, interesar
  12. Demonstratives, colours. Verb: Preferir.
  13. Tener que + infinitivo. Aficiones.
  14. Essay: Mi familia.
  15. Irregular verbos: Ir and Hacer.
  16. Irregular verbs with practice sentences.
  17. Revision of grammar.
  18. Revision of vocabulary, conversations and essays.
  19. Speaking and listening practice.
  20. Reading practice and mock test.
  21. Presentation: choose any 2 Hispanic countries and talk about them for 20 minutes. Practice exercises of the last 2 days’ syllabus.


24 Hour Medium Course:


  1. Expressing agreement/disagreement. Vocabulary: Clothes.
  2. Conversation in a shop. Cual/es. Lleva/n. Graduar.
  3. Introduction to: La Hora.
  4. Introduction to: Reflexive verbs with practice.
  5. Situar en el día, frequency.
  6. Essay: Mi rutina diaria.
  7. Vocabulary: Food and ingredients.
  8. Conversation in a restaurant. Verbs: poner & traer.
  9. Recipes and measurements.
  10. Ser vs Estar, Hay with exercises.
  11. Weather and quantifiers with exercises.
  12. Describing your neighbourhood with new adjectives and places.
  13. Giving and asking for directions with exercises.
  14. More quantifiers and describing what you most/least like – exercises.
  15. Essay: Mi barrio (My neighbourhood)
